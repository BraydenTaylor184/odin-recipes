Manicotti Recipe

Description

Manicotti is an Italian-American dish featuring large pasta tubes stuffed with a rich ricotta cheese filling, often accompanied by spinach and topped with marinara sauce and melted mozzarella. It’s a comforting, oven-baked meal that’s perfect for family dinners or holiday gatherings.

This recipe guides you through preparing homemade or store-bought manicotti shells filled with a creamy mixture and finished with bubbling cheese and a touch of fresh basil.

Ingredients

  • 14 manicotti shells
  • 15 oz ricotta cheese
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 egg
  • 1 cup chopped spinach (optional)
  • 1 jar (24 oz) marinara sauce
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil for garnish (optional)

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Cook manicotti shells according to package directions, then drain and cool.
  3. In a large bowl, mix ricotta, 1 1/2 cups mozzarella, Parmesan, egg, spinach, salt, and pepper.
  4. Spoon the filling into a piping bag or zip-top bag with the tip cut off.
  5. Gently fill each shell with the ricotta mixture.
  6. Spread a thin layer of marinara sauce on the bottom of a baking dish.
  7. Arrange the filled shells in the dish and cover with remaining sauce.
  8. Sprinkle remaining mozzarella cheese on top.
  9. Cover with foil and bake for 25 minutes, then remove foil and bake another 10 minutes until cheese is bubbly.
  10. Garnish with fresh basil before serving.
